What is cosplay?

Cosplay is a combination of two things; costumes and play. While "traditional" times to dress up, like Halloween, mostly involve a store bought costume for the sake of trick-or-treating or parties, Cosplay is so much more.

For many Cosplayers, a store bought costume just won't do. Countless hours go into the planning, preparation, and tailoring of their outfits. Whether they focus on sewing, foam work, or makeup, cosplayers create works of wearable art.



Cosplayers don't merely dress up like their favourite characters, they embody them. For the period of the convention, they put on a show. This is a vital part of the convention scene. The attention to detail goes beyond the costumes, and well into performances that delight punters of all ages.

Why cosplay?

Ask any cosplayer why they do it and the first answer will always be friends. By and large, the cosplay community is extremely welcoming. This kind of cummunity aspect to conventions, while not unique to the cosplayers, is an extremely important part of why people become cosplayers.


Not many hobbies encapsulate the learning curve of a cosplayer. The skills involved to make a screen-ready costume aren't learnt over night. Like any crafter or artist, these skills are constantly worked on, improved, and multiplied; that is to say most cosplayers will often branch out their skills.


Like the rest of us, some cosplayers are in for the competitions, too. The option to compete and show off their work with the chance of winning a prize is something that spans accross numerous hobbies. Cosplay is no different, to the point that some people work professionally as cosplayers.
